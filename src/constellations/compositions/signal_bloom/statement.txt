Ce n'est pas un arbre.

This is not drawn.
It is evaluated.

The work begins with a symbolic structure: a branching topology generated by\
an L-system and parsed into a tree. This structure contains no geometry. It \
is only relation—parent, child, depth, adjacency. A pure combinatorial objec\
t.

From this topology, a field of transformations is constructed.\

Each node in the tree carries a local function: a rotation in three-dimensio\
nal space, parameterized over a continuous variable t. These rotations are n\
ot constant. They activate over intervals, governed by smooth window functio\
ns, and are scaled by random angular coefficients. The result is a distribut\
ed system of latent motions—each node contributing a conditional transformat\
ion that may or may not express itself.

These local transformations are then composed downward through the tree.\

A traversal accumulates motion: parent transformations propagate into their \
children, forming a hierarchy of composed functions. What emerges is not a s\
ingle transformation, but a field—a mapping from position to behavior. Each \
point in space is influenced by a lineage of rotations encoded in the topolo\
gy.\

Geometry enters only afterward.\

Points are sampled across a disk and lifted into vertical lines—simple primi\
tives, identical in form. Each line is assigned to the nearest leaf in the t\
ree and inherits its corresponding transformation. The field is then applied\
, bending each line according to the accumulated rotations of its associated\
branch.

The image is the trace of this process.\

What appears as organic growth—a branching, blooming structure—is the visibl\
e residue of composed functions acting over a parameter space. The “bloom” i\
s not simulated as a plant; it is the consequence of transformations activat\
ing across depth and time.

The system is written in a custom programming language embedded in Python, b\
uilt on algebraic abstractions: functors, applicatives, monads, and arrows. \
These structures are not decorative—they determine how data flows, how funct\
ions compose, and how the final image is constructed.\

Each work is a finite projection of an infinite process:\

The tree is conceptually unbounded.\
The transformation field is continuous.\
The sampling is partial.\

What is shown is one resolution, one slice, one stabilization of a system th\
at could always be extended further.\

There is no single object being depicted.\

There is only the evaluation of structure into form.\

Ce n'est pas un arbre.
